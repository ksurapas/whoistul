Four years at Chula shaped how I think about the world. Economics isn't just about markets and models — it's a way of reasoning about trade-offs, incentives, and human behavior.

The program gave me a strong quantitative foundation: econometrics, statistics, and data analysis. But the real takeaway was learning to ask better questions. Why do people make the choices they make? What does the data actually tell us versus what we want it to say?

These are the questions I still carry into my work today.
