I travel to reset perspective. There's something about being in an unfamiliar place that makes you pay attention to things you'd normally overlook — the way a city sounds in the morning, how people move through public spaces, what the food tells you about the culture.

I prefer slow travel over checklists. Spending a few days in one neighborhood beats rushing through ten landmarks. Some of my favorite trips have been the least planned ones.

Southeast Asia has been my main playground so far, but I'm working on expanding the map.
