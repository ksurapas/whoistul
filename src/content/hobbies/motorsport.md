I've been following Formula 1 for years. What draws me in isn't just the speed — it's the data. F1 is one of the most data-intensive sports in the world. Teams make thousands of strategic decisions per race based on telemetry, tire models, weather data, and competitor analysis.

As a data analyst, I find the strategy layer fascinating. When to pit, which compound to run, how to manage tire degradation across a stint — these are optimization problems playing out in real time at 300 km/h.

Race weekends are a highlight of my calendar. There's nothing quite like watching a well-executed undercut or a bold strategy call that reshuffles the entire grid.
