There's something about working in consulting that rewires how you think. You stop seeing isolated problems and start seeing systems — loops, dependencies, feedback cycles.

## The Shift

Before joining PwC, I thought of problems as things to solve one at a time. Economics at Chula gave me the theoretical framework, but consulting gave me the practical lens. Every client engagement is a system: people, processes, data, incentives.

## Patterns Everywhere

Once you start thinking in systems, it's hard to stop. A slow dashboard isn't just a tech issue — it's a data pipeline issue, which is a process issue, which is an incentive issue. Pull one thread and the whole thing unravels.

## What I'm Learning

I've been reading "Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman, and it maps surprisingly well onto how I approach data analysis. System 1 gives you the gut feeling that something's off in the numbers. System 2 is the SQL query that proves it.

The best analysts I've worked with toggle between both effortlessly. That's what I'm working toward.
