I didn't plan to become a keyboard person. It just happened.

## The Gateway

The Keychron K2 was my first mechanical keyboard. I bought it because my laptop keyboard was giving me wrist pain. The moment I typed on it, something clicked — literally and figuratively. The tactile feedback, the sound, the build quality. It was a different experience entirely.

## The Escalation

Within six months I'd ordered a QK75 kit, watched hours of typing tests on YouTube, and developed opinions about gasket mount vs. top mount. I learned to lube switches, tune stabilizers, and debate the merits of PBT vs. ABS keycaps.

The Exclusive E7-V2 was the point of no return. A proper custom board with an aluminum case, brass weight, and a sound profile that I can only describe as "thocky." It's objectively too much keyboard for someone who mostly types emails and SQL queries.

## What I've Learned

The keyboard hobby is really about intentionality. You're choosing exactly how a tool feels and sounds. There's something satisfying about optimizing the thing you touch for 8+ hours a day.

Also, it's a gateway to other rabbit holes. Desk setups, cable management, artisan keycaps. The rabbit hole has rabbit holes.

## Current Daily Driver

The QK75 with Gateron Oil King switches and GMK keycaps. It's the sweet spot of sound, feel, and not-too-ridiculous price.
